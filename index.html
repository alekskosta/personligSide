- Klangre -

My fourth soloalbum "Klangre" is out now !
A few years ago, I read a research article about my hometown, Bodø, and global warming. It revealed that my city had lost 60 winter days over the past 30 years. By winter days, they meant days with temperatures below 0 degrees Celsius.
With this in mind, I created "Klangre", a tribute to our winter.

- About -

Aleksander Kostopoulos is an audiovisual drummer, composer, and artist from Bodø, Norway. He is known for his work in the band ISÁK, where he collaborated with producer James Daniel Njie Eriksen and vocalist Ella Marie Hætta Isaksen. As a solo artist, he creates cinematic electronic music and has released four albums, two audiovisual works, as well as composed music for dance performances, theater, and short films. In addition, Kostopoulos has played drums and percussion for various artists and bands, including Mari Boine, Adjagas, Kajsa Balto, Moddi, Marthe Valle, Julie Alapnes, and Pil & Bue.

He is a multidimensional artist who seamlessly blends music, technology, and visual art to create captivating experiences. As a composer and drummer, he crafts music that ranges from dynamic percussive landscapes to immersive electronic and ambient compositions. His passion for innovation extends into audiovisual art and creative development, where he combines coding, design and audiovisual concert installations.

- Audiovisual -

- Oftania -

An audiovisual concert-installation made in collaboration between drummer, composer and artist Aleksander Kostopoulos and light-designer Torbjørn Thrane Sandnes. It features a central cube within the room, around which the audience can stand or sit.

- FolaNero -

An audiovisual concert combining AI visuals and audio-reactive lamps, made in collaboration between drummer, composer, and artist Aleksander Kostopoulos and light designer Anders Søvik Hjelden.
